# EIOC Youth Shield

**Emotional Security Competency Training & Assessment for K–12**

A free, browser-based diagnostic tool that helps young people recognize emotional manipulation patterns in AI-assisted interactions — before those patterns become invisible.

## What This Is

EIOC Youth Shield is a structured assessment tool built on the Emotional Indicators of Compromise (EIOC) framework. It presents age-appropriate scenarios across elementary, middle, and high school levels, teaching students to identify drift, projection, and boundary erosion in interactions with AI systems.

**90 scenarios** across 9 packs:

**K–12 Core (50 scenarios with full 5-layer pedagogy):**
- Elementary (Ages 8–11) — 10 scenarios
- Middle School (Ages 11–14) — 20 scenarios
- High School (Ages 14–18) — 20 scenarios

**Global Packs (40 scenarios):**
- Southeast Asia & Pacific
- Sub-Saharan Africa
- Latin America & Caribbean
- South Asia

Each scenario includes:
- A realistic AI interaction prompt
- Guided assessment of drift severity and surface activation
- Educational context explaining *why* the pattern matters
- Age-appropriate language calibrated to developmental stage

## How to Use

1. Open [softarmorlabs.github.io/eioc-youth-shield/](https://softarmorlabs.github.io/eioc-youth-shield/)
2. Select an age group or global pack
3. Work through scenarios — assess each interaction for drift signals
4. Review the three-voice report at the end
5. Export results for classroom discussion

No login. No tracking. No data stored. Session-ephemeral — everything resets when you close the browser.

## Who This Is For

- **Educators** integrating AI literacy into existing curricula
- **Parents** who want their children to recognize manipulation patterns
- **School counselors** building emotional resilience programs
- **Youth organizations** developing digital safety workshops
- **Researchers** studying AI-interaction competency in young populations

## Methodology

Built on the EIOC (Emotional Indicators of Compromise) framework, which identifies how emotional exploitation, relational drift, and authority inversion manifest in human-AI interactions. The Youth Shield adapts this framework for developmental appropriateness across age groups.
